## Session Content

### What is Critical Thinking?

Critical thinking is the objective analysis and evaluation of an issue in order to form a judgment. It involves:

- **Analysis**: Breaking down complex problems into manageable parts
- **Evaluation**: Assessing the credibility and strength of evidence
- **Inference**: Drawing reasonable conclusions from available information
- **Interpretation**: Understanding meaning and significance
- **Explanation**: Articulating reasoning clearly

### Why It Matters

In our daily lives, we constantly make decisions based on incomplete information. Critical thinking helps us:

- Make better personal and professional decisions
- Avoid being misled by faulty reasoning
- Communicate more effectively
- Solve problems more systematically

### Key Components

1. **Asking the Right Questions**
2. **Gathering Relevant Information**
3. **Evaluating Sources and Evidence**
4. **Identifying Assumptions**
5. **Drawing Reasonable Conclusions**
