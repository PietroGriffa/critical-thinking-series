## Overview

Exploring common cognitive biases that affect our judgment and decision-making processes. This session builds on critical thinking fundamentals by examining how our minds can lead us astray.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

- Identify common cognitive biases in everyday thinking
- Understand the evolutionary origins of mental shortcuts
- Develop strategies to recognize and mitigate bias

## Session Content

### Understanding Mental Shortcuts

Our brains use heuristics (mental shortcuts) to make quick decisions, but these can sometimes lead to systematic errors in thinking.

### Common Cognitive Biases

#### Confirmation Bias
The tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information that confirms our pre-existing beliefs.

#### Anchoring Bias
Over-reliance on the first piece of information encountered when making decisions.

#### Availability Heuristic
Judging probability by how easily examples come to mind.

#### Dunning-Kruger Effect
The tendency for people with limited knowledge to overestimate their competence.

#### Sunk Cost Fallacy
Continuing an endeavor because of previously invested resources, rather than future value.

### Evolutionary Perspective

Many biases served important survival functions:
- Quick decision-making in dangerous situations
- Social cohesion and group survival
- Pattern recognition for threat detection

### Mitigation Strategies

1. **Slow Down**: Take time for important decisions
2. **Seek Disconfirming Evidence**: Actively look for information that challenges your views
3. **Consider Multiple Perspectives**: Ask "What would someone who disagrees think?"
4. **Use Systematic Approaches**: Employ decision-making frameworks
5. **Get External Input**: Seek feedback from others
