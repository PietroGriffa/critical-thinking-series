# Demo: ELM in Action - Analyzing Persuasive Messages

This demo helps participants understand how the Elaboration Likelihood Model works in real-world persuasion scenarios.

## Exercise 1: Central vs Peripheral Processing

Analyze these two advertisements and identify which route of persuasion they primarily use:

### Advertisement A: Technical Software
> "Our new productivity software increases efficiency by 34% based on a 6-month study of 500 companies. The algorithm optimizes workflow through advanced machine learning, reducing redundant tasks and improving team coordination. Independent testing shows 89% customer satisfaction with detailed performance metrics available upon request."

**Questions:**
- What route of persuasion is this using?
- What evidence suggests central processing?
- Who would be most likely to process this centrally vs peripherally?

### Advertisement B: Luxury Car
> "Experience the ultimate in luxury. Join celebrities and executives who choose excellence. Elegant design meets sophisticated performance. You deserve the best."

**Questions:**
- What route of persuasion is this using?
- What peripheral cues are being used?
- When might someone process this message centrally?

## Exercise 2: Personal Motivation and Ability

Consider these scenarios and predict which processing route people would likely use:

**Scenario 1**: A busy parent choosing between breakfast cereals while grocery shopping with crying children.

**Scenario 2**: A financial advisor researching investment options for a major client portfolio.

**Scenario 3**: A college student choosing a smartphone plan on a tight budget.

## Exercise 3: Identifying Your Own Processing

Think about a recent purchase or decision you made:

1. What was the decision?
2. How much time did you spend researching?
3. What factors influenced you most?
4. Which ELM route did you primarily use?
5. What circumstances led you to use that route?
